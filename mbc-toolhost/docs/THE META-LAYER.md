# I. ROUTERS (Flow-Directing Mechanisms)

Routers determine where and how information, distinctions, or operators travel inside the MBC system.  
They enact directed transitions between Boxes, tiers, and states.  
Routers are responsible for:

* adjacency  
* causation  
* polarity-driven selection  
* scale-aware movement  
* mode-specific flow  
* operator routing

Routers correspond primarily to the μ, λ, ψ, Θ, χ families.

## 1\. μ-Router (Adjacency Router)

* Determines which Boxes or nodes are “neighbors.”  
* Defines local topology of the system.  
* Controls micro-structure flow and access.

Function:  
“Who is next to whom in semantic or geometric space?”  
---

## 2\. λ-Router (Curvature Router)

* Bends flows based on curvature conditions.  
* Implements geometric deformation routing.

Function:  
“How should flows bend or warp given curvature?”  
---

## 3\. ψ-Router (Waveform Router)

* Routes oscillations, waves, modes, and harmonic flows.  
* Manages synchronization and resonance patterns.

Function:  
“How do oscillatory patterns propagate through structure?”  
---

## 4\. Θ-Router (Polarity Router)

* Routes based on polarity (positive/negative, real/imaginary, OA/IGS).  
* Performs meta-selection conditioned on modal identity.

Function:  
“Which direction should the flow choose based on polarity or sign?”  
---

## 5\. χ-Router (Evolution Router)

* Directs flows along temporal or evolutionary pathways.  
* Manages lineage, sequencing, and stepwise transformation.

Function:  
“What is the next state in the evolution chain?”

# II. LOGIC GATES (Conditional-Enactment Mechanisms)

Logic gates determine whether a given operator or transition is allowed to occur.  
They impose boundary logic, admissibility constraints, and modal reasoning.  
Logic gates correspond mainly to Π, Θ, Ω, and ρ families.

## 1\. Π-Gate (Evaluation Gate)

* Tests truth, admissibility, and ontological validity.  
* Governs whether a transformation is allowed.

Rule:  
“Does this operator satisfy the criteria for actualization?”  
This is the metaphysical equivalent of logical if.  
---

## 2\. Θ-Gate (Polarity Gate)

* Filters or modifies operations based on polarity.  
* A gate for modal logic (OA/IGS, \+/-, real/imag).

Rule:  
“Is this transformation compatible with the current polarity state?”  
---

## 3\. Ω-Gate (Normalization Gate)

* Enforces global-level admissibility constraints.  
* Prevents runaway, divergent, or incoherent states.

Rule:  
“Is this consistent with the global normalization map?”  
---

## 4\. ρ-Gate (Coherence Gate)

* The highest-level gate—final admissibility.  
* Enforces the complete structural integrity of the entire system.

Rule:  
“Is this transition compatible with full-system coherence?”  
This gate is final and overrides all others.  
Nothing passes through ρ unless it is structurally coherent across all tiers.
