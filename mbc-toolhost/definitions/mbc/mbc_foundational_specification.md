# 3\. MBC Structural Components

MBC consists of six major structural components:

### 3.1 Boxes (Bxₙ)

The primary semantic units of computation.  
Boxes are:

* stabilized loops of Connectors  
* bounded semantic identities  
* “semantic solitons”  
* activated, structured portions of the semantic manifold

A Box has:

* ports  
* states  
* operators  
* invariants  
* connectors  
* relations

### 3.2 Connectors (Cₙ)

The fundamental relational edges that define structure.  
A Connector has four intrinsic vectors:

* Directionality (d)  
* Tension (T)  
* Polarity (P)  
* Conductivity (σ)

Connectors can:

* transfer semantic flux  
* accumulate tension  
* invert polarity  
* fuse into circuits  
* collapse under stress (MSC governs this)

### 3.3 Operators (Opₙ)

Operators act on Boxes.  
Each belongs to exactly one Tier (01–13) depending on its function.  
Operators can:

* transform  
* project  
* combine  
* rewrite  
* integrate  
* normalize  
* amplify  
* collapse  
* evaluate  
* route

### 3.4 Rewrite Systems (Rₙ)

Symbolic and structural transformation rules.  
Rewrite systems handle:

* normalization  
* reduction to canonical forms  
* elimination of redundancy  
* error correction  
* structural repair

Rewrite engines ensure semantic integrity.

### 3.5 Module Packs (Modₙ)

Groupings of operators \+ rewrites \+ invariants forming functional “subsystems.”  
Examples:

* Combiner Modules  
* Oscillation Modules  
* Filter Chains  
* Semantic Normalizers  
* Amplification Circuits

Module packs allow MBC to behave like a programmable semantic CPU.

### 3.6 Axiom Boxes & Interaction Tables

* Axiom Boxes define structural truths.  
* Interaction Tables define legal operator interactions.

Together they create a rule-bound computational environment.
