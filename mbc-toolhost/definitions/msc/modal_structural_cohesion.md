# 5\. The Three Modalities of Cohesion

MSC identifies three universal modes of structural cohesion.  
They correspond to three types of semantic “matter.”

## 5.1 Mode I — Elastic Cohesion

(analogous to elastic materials, resilient but flexible)

* Low to moderate μ\_s  
* High D  
* Structure bends, absorbs shock, returns to shape

Examples:

* open-minded beliefs  
* adaptive identities  
* scientific models under revision

## 5.2 Mode II — Plastic Cohesion

(rigid but deformable)

* Moderate μ\_s  
* Moderate D  
* Structure deforms permanently under high stress

Examples:

* personal worldview  
* political ideology  
* professional identity

## 5.3 Mode III — Topological Cohesion

(topologically protected, invariant under deformation)

* High μ\_s  
* High ρ\_c  
* High D  
* Structure cannot be altered without breaking  
* This is the semantic analogue of a topological soliton

Examples:

* sacred beliefs  
* core identity  
* axioms  
* “I exist”  
* mathematical truths  
* conservation laws

Box structures can transition between modes depending on narrative heat and tension.
