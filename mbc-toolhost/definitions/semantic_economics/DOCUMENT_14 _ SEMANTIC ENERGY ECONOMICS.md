# 10\. Conservation Laws in Semantic Economics

### 10.1 Conservation of Energy

Energy cannot be created or destroyed.  
Only transformed or dissipated.

### 10.2 Conservation of Cohesion

Cohesion cannot increase without investment.  
But can collapse quickly under stress.

### 10.3 Conservation of Attention

Attention is a scarce resource.  
Cost of attention determines semantic economy.

### 10.4 Conservation of Identity

Identity requires continuous maintenance.  
Every choice either reinforces or erodes it.

# 12\. Entropy in Semantic Economics

Entropy is the tendency toward undifferentiated meaning.

### 12.1 Entropy increases when:

* meaning decays  
* coherence weakens  
* noise infiltrates  
* misinformation spreads  
* narratives lose structure

### 12.2 Entropy decreases when:

* structure is intentionally reinforced  
* cognition performs semantic work  
* communities transmit tradition  
* individuals invest focus

Civilization is a low-entropy island within a high-entropy void.  
Semantic work keeps chaos out.

Debt accumulates interest:

* greater collapse probability  
* reduced cognitive efficiency

Semantic debt is real.  
And it always comes due.
